About me

My name is Laura Sirvent Collado and I'm a Spain based Character Animator and Multimedia Engineer.

Since I was a kid I spent a lot of time using (and spoiling) my dad's computer, trying to figure out how videogames and applications were made, dreaming of "doing" my owns. Those feelings were added to filmmaking when in 1995 -maybe a couple years later- Pixar's Toy Story was released and I fell deeply in love with it, and with the idea of living toys, so I started "shoting" (with a very homemade stop-motion technique) my owns "films".

After finishing high school I studied a Bachelor in Multimedia Engineering in Universidad de Alicante (Spain), and I got solved almost of all my questions and thoughts about videogames and applications development. There I got programming, web development, videogames development, sound and video theory, apps development, and other "multimedia" theories and techniques knowledges, and I worked at many group and personal projects, such as videogames, desktop applications, web apps and mobile apps. I almost knew about what I wanted; except for one more thing: filmmaking.

Just a week after getting my last grades I started a course in 3D Character Animation, Modelling and Storytelling at Pepe-School-Land in Barcelona. I learnt a lot of animation techniques, the twelve principles, acting, staging and all those stuff. I also got basic knowledges about storytelling, and 3D modelling for production. From this course I got an early demo reel I hope it's a good starting point.

Now I'm improving my animation skills by making short actings and performances, and I'm looking for any job opportunity that can make me work with motivation and improve my skills.

From then to now, this is me.

The Algorithmic Composer (Summer 2014)

The Algorithmic Composer is an algorithmic composition application made by me as my final degree project. It has implementation of many music compositions methods.

The application borns as an idea from my final degree tutor Jose Manuel Iñesta to develop a program capable of showing the strengh and possibilities of algorithmic composition for people who needs it’s own music for their projects. He ask me to make an application that contains basic implementations of the principals algorithmic composition methods. I started development at firsts of June of 2014 and I’m finishing a very very early version on finals of August. It has a lot of options and possibilities as well as lacks of methods and new ideas that wasn’t developed because of the deadline.

Noma (2013-2014)

Experimental videogame for OSX developed with teammate Raúl Ibarra. Noma is the lonely inhabitant of a planet, where nobody appears to be. Then he starts a travel looking for answers, until one day, when he finds a monolith...

Developed from zero in nine months, implementing the game logic, AI, graphics engine, sound engine and UI. The language used is C++, audio library irrKlang, physics library Bullet, graphics engine made over OpenGL.

All the 3D models were fully modelled, textured, rigged and animated by us in Blender.

We also made a small Teaser using camera tracking techniques, and a motion graphics making of ;)

La Caja Fuerte (Fall 2013)

Videogame for Amstrad CPC developed in two weeks with teammate Raúl Ibarra, for the I Concurso de Desarrollo de Videojuegos Retro de la Universidad de Alicante, where we won the prize for the Most Creative Videogame.

The rules of the contest said we had to develope the videogame as we would be in the late 80s, without any modern technologies or languages converters, so we started learning BASIC as we developed the game.

As the topic of the contest was robbers, the main idea of the videogame is to steal the safe of a bank. The game is intended to be played by to players at the same time.
